Irodori—What can you do?

Irodori is the Japan Foundation's free, wonderfully down-to-earth course for actually living life in Japan. Great stuff, but if you've already got some Japanese under your belt, trudging from lesson one is nobody's idea of a good time.

Have a skim through each skill (the book talks about "can-do's") and sentence pattern: got it, a bit rusty, or totally new? At the end you'll get a tidy little cheat-sheet of what to skip, what to review, and what's worth actually sitting down to study.

If at any point you feel like you don't really know any of the question, you can just stop and !

All the information was compiled from the resources the creators of Irodori put together, only restructured to fit this questionairre. AI was used to translate and provide descriptions and examples, by strictly following my specifications. Feel free to check out the code repository.
